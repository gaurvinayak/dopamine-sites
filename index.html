Frequently Asked Questions

What is a dopamine site?
A dopamine site is a fake interactive app that simulates a high-stakes or satisfying real-world experience — ordering food, booking a train ticket, getting a job offer — and delivers the psychological reward without any real commitment. The term comes from how these sites trigger the same anticipation-reward loop as the real apps.
Are these safe to use? Do they collect any data?
Yes, completely safe. These are static HTML pages with no backend, no database, no accounts, and no data collection. Everything runs entirely in your browser. Nothing you type is sent anywhere.
Do these work on mobile?
Yes. All 10 sites are mobile-first and work in any modern browser on Android or iPhone. No app download needed.

Most dopamine sites are built for Western audiences — fake Amazon, fake Twitter, fake Starbucks. These are built around experiences that every Indian internet user recognizes: IRCTC Tatkal at 10 AM, Swiggy at 1 AM, Zerodha P&L refresh, the Chai Tapri break, the FAANG offer daydream. The specific trauma and joy are distinctly desi.
